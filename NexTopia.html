NexTopia

Co-designing Attainable Utopias

Revolutionizing Urban Planning by Democratizing the Design Process Through the Web

Project Overview

NexTopia creates a digital ecosystem that empowers citizens of all ages, with special emphasis on children and seniors, to collaboratively reimagine and reshape their urban environments using open city data.

Drawing from Henri Lefebvre's influential theory on "The Social Production of Space" (1991), NexTopia recognizes that the most vibrant urban spaces have always been shaped by diverse human experiences. As Lefebvre argues, space is not merely a neutral container but a social product created through collective human action and imagination.

Inspired by Marji et al.'s research on speculative design and fantastical cities (2023), NexTopia challenges the traditional notion that urban planning must be limited to practical constraints. Instead, we harness the power of imagination to envision what Marji describes as "attainable utopias" — urban spaces that balance visionary thinking with implementable solutions.

"Architecture and the urban environment have always been fertile terrain for human imagination." — Marji et al. (2023)

The platform creates immersive virtual replicas of participating cities where citizens manipulate, design, and visualize urban space according to their needs, fears, and aspirations — transforming the question of "what our cities could be" into "who gets to design them."

Key Features

Intergenerational Approach

Brings together imaginative visions of children and lived experience of older adults, creating a unique dynamic that bridges generational perspectives and values.

Attainable Utopias

Not mere digital fantasies — analytical tools translate creative inputs into practical specifications, cost estimates, and implementation roadmaps.

Open Source, Accessible, Cross-Platform

Built on open city data APIs with accessible design for all technical skill levels, ensuring inclusive participation across the digital divide.

Immersive Virtual Replicas

Digital twins of participating cities allow citizens to manipulate, design, and visualize urban spaces according to their needs, fears, and aspirations.

Collaborative Design Tools

Multi-layered tools with varying complexity levels accommodate different user capabilities while maintaining inclusivity across generations.

Analytical Transformation

Advanced tools transform citizen designs into actionable urban planning proposals and feasibility studies for real-world implementation.

Implementation Framework

The NexTopia project follows a structured 36-month implementation framework divided into four progressive phases:

1) Foundation (Months 1-6)

  • Establish consortium partnerships across municipalities, technology providers, and research institutions
  • Develop technical specifications for the NexTopia platform
  • Conduct user research with diverse stakeholder groups across generations
  • Create governance framework for city participation and data sharing
  • Establish ethical guidelines for participant engagement, especially for children and elderly
  • Develop assessment metrics for measuring impact and success

2) Platform Development (Months 7-18)

  • Develop core NextTopia web platform with modular architecture
  • Integrate open city data APIs from partner municipalities
  • Create intuitive, accessible interface designed for all ages
  • Develop visualization engine for urban spaces
  • Build collaborative design tools with varying complexity levels
  • Implement analytical tools to transform designs into actionable plans

3) Pilot Implementation (Months 19-30)

  • Launch in five diverse European cities (urban, suburban, post-industrial)
  • Train local facilitators in each pilot city
  • Implement community engagement strategies targeting intergenerational participation
  • Organize guided co-creation workshops in schools and senior centers
  • Collect and analyze user feedback for platform refinement
  • Document emerging design patterns and community priorities
  • Select top citizen-designed projects for feasibility studies

4) Translation to Reality (Months 31-36)

  • Transform selected virtual designs into detailed implementation plans
  • Engage urban planners and architects to refine citizen designs
  • Secure municipal commitments for implementing at least one design per city
  • Develop sustainability plan for platform maintenance beyond project lifecycle
  • Create knowledge transfer toolkit for new cities

Pilot Use Cases

Coastal Urban Innovation

Focus on revitalizing coastal urban areas balancing tourism and local quality of life.

The Challenge:

Target Groups:

Local schools, elderly residents, tourism industry workers.

The Challenge:

Addressing isolation, mobility issues, and changing climate conditions.

Target Groups:

Schools, senior housing communities, commuter populations.

Expected Outcomes:

Youth-elder co-designed virtual community hubs translated to implementation plans.

Arctic Urban Development

Oulu, Finland

Designing climate-adaptive urban spaces for extreme conditions.

The Challenge:

Creating vibrant public spaces usable year-round despite harsh winters.

Target Groups:

University students, elderly citizens, tech industry professionals.

Expected Outcomes:

Climate-resilient urban design templates adaptable to extreme conditions with simulation tools visualizing microclimatic impacts.

Expected Impact

Research shows that collaborative intergenerational urban design leads to more resilient, inclusive, and sustainable cities. NexTopia aims to create measurable impacts across multiple dimensions:

Democratizing Urban Design

Shifting from top-down planning to genuine co-creation, giving voice to traditionally underrepresented groups.

Bridging Generational Divides

Creating meaningful collaboration between young and old citizens, enhancing mutual understanding and respect.

Enhancing Digital Literacy

Providing accessible digital tools that improve technical competencies across generations.

Strengthening Community Bonds

Fostering ownership and participation in local urban development.

Improving Urban Sustainability

Leveraging collective intelligence to identify creative solutions rooted in lived experience.

Increasing Government Transparency

Creating direct feedback loops between citizens and authorities on urban development.

Measurable Outcomes

  • Minimum of 3 citizen-designed projects approved for implementation
  • 50%+ increase in reported trust in local governance process
  • 30%+ increase in urban design literacy among participants
  • Documented improvement in intergenerational understanding and collaboration
  • Adoption of NexTopia methodology by at least 3 non-pilot cities

Theoretical Underpinnings

NexTopia builds upon a rich theoretical foundation that combines urban theory, speculative design, and intergenerational research:

Lefebvre's Social Production of Space

Henri Lefebvre's seminal work "The Production of Space" (1991) established that space is not merely a neutral container but a social product created through collective human action. NexTopia operationalizes Lefebvre's concept by creating digital tools that democratize the production of urban space, recognizing that cities are shaped by the lived experiences of their inhabitants.

"Space is permeated with social relations; it is not only supported by social relations but it is also producing and produced by social relations." — Henri Lefebvre, The Production of Space (1991)

Marji's Research on Fantastical Cities

Narmeen Marji's work on "A Typology of Fantastical Cities and Architecture" (2023) explores how imagining fantastic and future cities has played a key role in developing Urban Design as a discipline. NexTopia draws from this research to create a space where imaginative visions can be channeled into practical urban solutions.

In "Blueprints of Tomorrow: Co-Designing Speculative Urban Futures" (2023), Marji et al. provide a framework for utilizing speculative design in participatory urban planning, which directly informs NexTopia's methodological approach.

Intergenerational Public Space Design

Recent research on intergenerational public space design (SAGE Journals, 2022) has shown that spaces designed with input from both children and seniors tend to be more accessible, inclusive, and socially vibrant. NexTopia's focus on bringing together children and seniors in the design process is directly informed by this body of research.

Research Through Design

NexTopia employs a Research Through Design methodology, where the act of creating digital urban interventions generates new knowledge about collaborative urban planning. This approach allows us to simultaneously develop innovative tools while advancing scholarly understanding of participatory urban design.
